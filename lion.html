African Lion

What is the African Lion?

It is the second-largest living big cat after the tiger. Males are unique among the cat species for their thick mane of brown or black hair encircling their head and neck. The mane darkens with age, and the thicker and darker a mane is the healthier the cat. Both males and females roar â€” a sound heard as far as 8 kilometers away.

Characteristic Details
Conservation Status Vulnerable
Scientific Name Panthera Leo
Weight 125 to 272 kilograms (277 to 600 pounds)
Size 1.2 meters at the shoulder (48 inches) and about 2 to 3.3 meters in length (7 to 11 feet)
Life Span 10 to 18 years in the wild; up to 30 years in captivity
Habitat Grassy plains and open woodlands
Diet Carnivorous
Gestation Average about 109 days
Predators Humans
